Troubleshooting & Resources

Fix Your Prints & Find Free Files.

Do not let a bad print stop your momentum. When your printer acts up, use these quick-reference fixes to get back on track.

01

The First Layer Failure

If filament is not sticking to the bed, the nozzle is likely too high. Level the bed, clean the build surface, and run a one-layer leveling test.

02

Stringing and Cobwebs

If prints look hairy, retraction settings or temperature may be too high. Lower the nozzle temperature by 5°C, test again, and adjust retraction gradually.

03

Open-Source File Library

Use vetted STL files for calibration cubes, stress tests, bed-level checks, and useful starter prints without modeling from scratch.

Change one setting at a time.

A clean troubleshooting process is faster than changing several settings at once. Save a known-good profile, make one adjustment, and print a small test object before starting a long project.

  • Confirm bed level and first-layer height
  • Check material temperature range
  • Inspect retraction and travel settings
  • Keep notes on successful profiles
